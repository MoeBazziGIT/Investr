What is Investing?
Formal definition: The act of committing money or capital to an endeavor with the expectation of obtaining an additional income or profit.
But wait, what is capital? Well, capital is just a term for financial assets or wealth. For most people, it refers to money held at the bank that’s ready to be used. In other words, investing is the process of laying out money now to receive more money in the future. To make this extra money, the capital is put into certain ‘investment vehicles’.
Investment vehicles don’t refer to literal vehicles. In their purest form, they are just products used by investors with the intention of gaining positive returns. They can vary in terms of risk, return, time horizon, as well as features. You have definitely heard of examples of these vehicles before. They include stocks, fixed-income investments (bonds and GICs), and mutual funds. We will go more into detail on those a bit later.
A very important concept of investing is the concept of risk versus return. In general, there is a concrete relationship between the two, which means that the riskier a certain investment is, the higher potential return. To illustrate this, assume two investment vehicles: Option A with a potential return of 10% and potential loss of 5%, and Option B with a potential return of 12% and a potential loss of 7%. If both options had the same potential loss of 5%, everyone would obviously go with option B. But in reality, option B has to offer a higher return than option A because it comes with a potential to lose more money. This is referred to as a risk premium, which is essentially a reward for carrying on more risk. Let’s look more into this concept of risk and how to reduce it.
